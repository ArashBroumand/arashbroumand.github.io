Problem:
Show that the minimum-Bayes-risk function \( V \) (defined in section II.C of Vincent Poor's Signal Detection and Estimation) is concave and continuous on \( [0,1] \).
[After showing that \( V \) is concave, you may use the fact that any concave function on \( [0,1] \) is continuous on \( (0,1) \).]

Solution:

\( V(\pi_0) = \pi_0 R_0(\delta_{\pi_0}) + (1-\pi_0) R_1(\delta_{\pi_0}) \)

\( \forall \pi_0, 0\leq \pi_0 \leq 1; \exists \alpha, \pi_1, \pi_2, 0 \leq \alpha, \pi_1, \pi_2 \leq 1, \) so that \( \pi_0 = \alpha \pi_1 + (1 - \alpha) \pi_2 \) \[ \begin{array}{l l} V(\pi_0) & = V(\alpha \pi_1 + (1 - \alpha) \pi_2) \\\\ & = [\alpha \pi_1 + (1 - \alpha) \pi_2] R_0(\delta_{\pi_0}) + [1-(\alpha \pi_1 + (1 - \alpha) \pi_2)]R_1(\delta_{\pi_0}) \\\\ & = [\alpha \pi_1 + (1 - \alpha) \pi_2] R_0(\delta_{\pi_0}) + [\alpha + (1-\alpha)-(\alpha \pi_1 + (1 - \alpha) \pi_2)]R_1(\delta_{\pi_0}) \\\\ & = \alpha \pi_0 R_0(\delta_{\pi_0}) + (1-\alpha)\pi_2 R_0(\delta_{\pi_0}) + \alpha (1-\pi_1) R_1(\delta_{\pi_0}) + (1-\alpha)(1-\pi_2)R_1(\delta_{\pi_0})\\\\ & = \alpha [\pi_1 R_0(\delta_{\pi_0})+(1-\pi_1)R_1(\delta_{\pi_0})] + (1-\alpha) [ \pi_2 R_0(\delta_{\pi_0}) + (1-\pi_2) R_1(\delta_{\pi_0}) ] \\\\ & = \alpha r(\pi_1, \delta_{\pi_0}) + (1-\alpha) r(\pi_2, \delta_{\pi_0}) \\\\ \end{array} \] \( r(\pi_j,\delta_{\pi_0}) \) is the average risk of \( \delta_{\pi_0} \) rule, if the prior is actually \( \pi_j \). Since the Bayes rule is optimum, \( r(\pi_j, \delta_{\pi_0}) \geq r(\pi_j, \delta_{\pi_j}) = V(\pi_j), \forall \pi_0 \). So,
\[ V(\alpha \pi_1 + (1 - \alpha) \pi_2) \geq \alpha V(\pi_1) + (1-\alpha) V(\pi_2) \] i.e., \( V(\pi) \) is concave on \( [0,1] \). Since it is a concave function, it is also continuous on \( [0,1] \).
